Instructions

This wizard will guide you through planning out the rest of your studies for a major or minor in the department of Physics and Astronomy at UWEC. It does not account for liberal education requirements (yet), and obviously won't account for your second major or minor.

Here's how to use it:

  1. Select which degree plan you are pursuing in the "Degree" dropdown menu.
  2. Mark all courses you have already completed in prior terms by toggling the "Completed" switches for those classes, or by clicking the "completed" button in the "Details" dialog.
  3. Mark all courses you are enrolling in, or already taking, in the current term by toggling the "Enrolling" switch for these courses.
  4. Advance to the next tern by clicking the green button next to the "Term" indicator, or by selecting a new term from the dropdown menu.
  5. Continue to mark which courses you will enroll in for those subsequent terms, and repeat.

As you mark courses enrolled or completed, they will appear in your course plan at the bottom of the page.

More information

Courses in gold are available for you to take in the selected term. You have met the requirements for them by that time, and they are offered (though PHYS 486 has a more nebulous requirement, so it may appear prematurely available).

Courses in blue are courses you are enrolling in for the designated term. If you advance to a later term, they will turn green, indicating that you have completed them.

Courses that are gray are unavailable. They may require a corequisite class (ex. PHYS 231 requires prior or concurrent enrollment in MATH 114), a prerequisite class (ex. PHYS 232 requires you to have already completed PHYS 231), they may require a more complex set of courses (ex. PHYS 486 requires any two of PHYS 340, 350, and 360), and they may not be offered in that particular term (ex. PHYS 375 is only offered during the Spring of even years). To find out the requirements for a course, click on its "Details" button.

Finally, courses with a thicker border are required for the selected degree plan. They may be outright required, or they may be part of a selection of courses that you may opt to take. For instance, Applied Physics emphasis majors must take both PHYS 255 and PHYS 356, or they make take either of PHYS 365 or PHYS 375. Once any of these three requirements are met, the other courses will no longer be indicated as required.
